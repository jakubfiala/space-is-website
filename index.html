The Space Is In Between Us is a sound and movement performance frame for exploring togetherness. It takes place in non-theatre spaces, ideally an office, shop front or hall. The audience experiences the work through being invited to arrange a collection of objects and plants at particular intervals over the duration of 1-4 hours. Seats are placed in response to the composition in a way that provokes new perspectives. Each performance is bespoke, unique to the audiences present.

PERFORMANCES

We make experimental, durational improvisation performances with sound and movement, and these works are places for meeting. We are curious about the limits and possibilities of sound, of movement, of presence, of listening and observing. We use improvisation as a methodology to deliberately reveal, share and re–position decision making.

The durational improvisation performances series ‘a dog may or may not have barked’ (over 2013–14) was a focused exploration of movement and sound relations with unique perspectives through the reflective windows of a former BBC office.

We expanded our practice to more directly address connection with audience and space. Following on from ‘a dog...’, we continue to offer and respond to impulses from each other and the performance environment, but include the audience in creating the performance environment in order to involve them, and to make a direct connection. In our first series of ‘The Space Is In Between Us’ we performed 21 nights over November 2014, in a former office. Throughout these performances we meet and respond to audiences who all seek us out to play, challenge, and risk. As members of an intimate audience group, they observe and are observed, there's no place to only spectate.

Born in Sheffield, England. Works as a dance and movement artist, educator and Certified Movement Analyst (CMA), in London, Belgium and Edinburgh since 2004. Her experiences and practice include performing, choreographing, movement directing, teaching and writing. Artists she has on–going performance collaborations with include choreographer Mark Carberry, visual artists Moi Tran, and Mette Sterre, language artist Jane Frances Dunlop and photographer Mira Loew, and video artists Yuri Pirondi and Ines von Bonhorst of Magma Collective. Notable previous collaborations include polymath Ben Gwalchmai and visual artist Ben Varney. Alexandra has performed for various choreographers including Carolyn Deby/sirenscrossing, Athina Vahla and Manifold Motion. Her work as a movement director for theatre and opera includes Theatre Delicatessen, Hobo, MolinoGroup, UNTitled Theatre and Barefoot Opera. Alexandra has an ongoing commitment to and interest in collaborative practice.

alexandrabaybutt.co.uk

David is a Hungarian guitarist, composer and sound artist, currently based in London. In his artistic practice he focuses on site/space–specific performance and composition. He usually works in between different disciplines such as improvisation performance, sound art and video. His recent work includes the durational sound performance ‘Length of a Distance’ composed for the Greenwich Foot Tunnel, the experimental film ‘My sister’s dad’ and solo guitar album ‘Movement’.

David frequently works with dance/physical theatre as a composer, musician and collaborator. He worked with dancers/choreographers including Gary Clarke, Zsuzsa Rozsavolgyi (ex-Rosas), Dragana Alfirevic (NomadDanceAcademy) and Imre Vass (ex-UltimaVez) and performed these pieces in 10 European countries.
